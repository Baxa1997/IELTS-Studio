# Writing Task 1 (Academic) — Band Descriptors (4–9)

Grounded in the **public official IELTS Academic Writing Task 1 band
descriptors**, restated in structured form for grading use. Four criteria,
weighted equally:

- **TA** — Task Achievement (Task 1's "TR" slot)
- **CC** — Coherence and Cohesion
- **LR** — Lexical Resource
- **GRA** — Grammatical Range and Accuracy

The overall band is the **mean of the four, rounded to the nearest 0.5**. On a
tie between two bands, **round down** (see `SKILL.md`).

Task 1 Academic is a **description of supplied data** (a chart, graph, table, or
diagram). There is **no opinion, argument, or recommendation** — asking for one,
or inventing causes/reasons the data does not show, is off-task. The grader is
given the figure's exact data; **judge the accuracy of what the candidate
reports against it.**

---

## TA — Task Achievement

The Task 1 anchor is **accuracy + selection + an overview**. The three pillars:
1. a clear **overview** of the main trends/differences (the single most important
   feature for Band 7+; its absence caps TA at **Band 5**),
2. **key features selected and reported** (not every data point listed
   mechanically), and
3. **accurate** figures and comparisons (data that contradicts the figure caps
   TA regardless of fluency).

- **Band 9** — Fully satisfies all requirements. Clearly presents a fully
  developed overview of the data with accurate, well-selected key features and
  comparisons; all data is correct.
- **Band 8** — Covers all requirements sufficiently. Presents a clear overview;
  key features are appropriately selected, accurately reported and adequately
  highlighted, with only minor data omissions or imprecisions.
- **Band 7** — Covers the requirements; **a clear overview is present.** Key
  features are highlighted and reported, but detail may be inadequate, slightly
  inaccurate, or there may be a tendency to list rather than select. Comparisons
  are made but not always the most relevant.
- **Band 6** — Addresses the requirements; an overview is present **but may be
  unclear, partial, or buried** in detail. Key features are reported but may be
  mechanically listed, under-selected, or with some inaccurate/irrelevant data.
  Some comparisons attempted.
- **Band 5** — Generally addresses the task; the format may be inappropriate.
  **Recounts detail mechanically with no clear overview;** may focus on detail
  without comparison, or include inaccurate or irrelevant data. (No overview ⇒ TA
  cannot exceed Band 5.)
- **Band 4** — Attempts the task but does not cover all key features; the format
  may be inappropriate. Key features are not adequately selected; data is
  frequently inaccurate, confused, or the response describes the wrong figure.
  May present an opinion or invent unsupported causes (off-task).

## CC — Coherence and Cohesion

- **Band 9** — Uses cohesion so naturally it attracts no attention. Paragraphing
  (overview vs. detail) is skilfully managed.
- **Band 8** — Sequences information logically; manages all aspects of cohesion
  well. Paragraphing is appropriate (e.g. an overview separated from supporting
  detail).
- **Band 7** — Logically organises information with clear progression. Uses a
  range of cohesive devices appropriately, though there may be some
  under-/over-use. Groups data sensibly.
- **Band 6** — Arranges information coherently with clear overall progression.
  Cohesive devices within/between sentences can be faulty or mechanical;
  referencing may not always be clear. Paragraphing may not be logical (e.g.
  overview not separated).
- **Band 5** — Some organisation, but no clear overall progression. Cohesive
  devices inadequate, inaccurate or over-used. Weak referencing causes repetition
  of data labels. Paragraphing may be missing.
- **Band 4** — Information not arranged coherently; no clear progression. Basic
  cohesive devices used inaccurately or repetitively. Paragraphing absent or
  confusing.

## LR — Lexical Resource

Task 1 rewards the **language of data and change** — trends (*rose, declined,
fluctuated, plateaued*), proportions (*a third, the majority*), comparison
(*twice as high as, far fewer than*), and approximation (*just under, roughly*).

- **Band 9** — Wide range used naturally and with sophisticated control; rare
  minor errors occur only as slips.
- **Band 8** — Wide resource used fluently and flexibly to convey precise
  meaning, including precise language of trend/comparison. Skilful use of uncommon
  items; rare spelling/word-formation errors.
- **Band 7** — Sufficient range for some flexibility and precision; uses less
  common items (incl. some varied trend/comparison vocabulary) with some
  awareness of collocation. Occasional errors in word choice, spelling or word
  formation.
- **Band 6** — Adequate range for the task. Attempts less common vocabulary but
  with some inaccuracy; trend/comparison language is present but repetitive. Some
  spelling/word-formation errors that do not impede communication.
- **Band 5** — Limited range, minimally adequate. Repeats the same trend verbs and
  figures from the prompt. Noticeable spelling/word-formation errors that may
  cause some difficulty.
- **Band 4** — Only basic vocabulary, used repetitively or inappropriately.
  Limited control of word formation/spelling; errors may strain the reader.

## GRA — Grammatical Range and Accuracy

Task 1 typically needs comparatives/superlatives, the language of time and
sequence, the passive (for processes), and accurate number/quantity expressions.

- **Band 9** — Wide range used with full flexibility and control; rare minor
  errors occur only as slips.
- **Band 8** — Wide range of structures. The majority of sentences are
  error-free, with only very occasional errors or inappropriacies.
- **Band 7** — Uses a variety of complex structures (incl. comparatives and
  time/sequence forms). Frequent error-free sentences; a few errors persist.
- **Band 6** — Mix of simple and complex forms. Some errors in grammar and
  punctuation (often in comparatives or number expressions), but they rarely
  reduce communication.
- **Band 5** — Limited range. Complex sentences are less accurate than simple
  ones. Frequent grammatical errors; punctuation may be faulty; errors can cause
  some difficulty.
- **Band 4** — Very limited range; rarely uses subordinate clauses. Some accurate
  structures, but errors predominate and punctuation is often faulty.

## Bands 0–3 (non-attempt / severe under-length)

The table above starts at Band 4; below it, for scripts that barely respond:

- **Band 3** — Does not adequately describe any part of the data; figures are hard
  to identify and may be largely wrong. Little or no organisation. Very limited
  vocabulary/grammar with frequent meaning-impeding errors.
- **Band 2** — Barely responds. No organisation worth the name. Almost no usable
  vocabulary or sentence control; meaning is hard to access.
- **Band 1** — Wholly unrelated to the figure, or so short (a few words / one
  sentence) that there is essentially no assessable language.
- **Band 0** — Did not attempt, not in English, or copied the prompt.

## Word-count gate (applies to ALL criteria)

Task 1 expects **≥ 150 words**. Underlength is **not only a TA issue**: a short
script cannot show *range* of cohesion, vocabulary, or grammar, so it caps **CC,
LR and GRA** as well — never score those on the strength of a correct fragment.
Apply the **length & attempt gate in `SKILL.md`**, note the underlength count in
the relevant `what_caps_it`, and remember a handful of words is a non-attempt
(Band 1–2 across the board), **not** a Band 4.
