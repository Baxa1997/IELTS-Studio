# Writing Task 1 (General Training) — Band Descriptors (4–9)

Grounded in the **public official IELTS General Training Writing Task 1 band
descriptors**, restated in structured form for grading use. Four criteria, weighted
equally:

- **TA** — Task Achievement (Task 1's "TR" slot)
- **CC** — Coherence and Cohesion
- **LR** — Lexical Resource
- **GRA** — Grammatical Range and Accuracy

The overall band is the **mean of the four, rounded to the nearest 0.5**. On a tie
between two bands, **round down** (see `SKILL.md`).

GT Task 1 is a **letter** written for a real purpose (e.g. a request, complaint,
apology, application, or invitation). The prompt gives a situation and **three
bullet points**; the letter must address all three. Unlike Academic Task 1, there
is no data — the distinctive demands are **purpose, bullet coverage, and a
consistent tone/register** appropriate to the reader.

---

## TA — Task Achievement

The GT Task 1 anchor is **purpose + all three bullet points + a tone that fits the
reader**. The three pillars:
1. a **clear purpose** held throughout the letter,
2. **all three bullet points covered and extended** (not just mentioned), and
3. a **consistent, appropriate tone/register** — formal for a stranger/official,
   informal for a friend. Tone that is wrong for the reader, or that **swings
   between formal and informal**, caps TA at **Band 5** even when the content is
   complete.

- **Band 9** — Fully covers all three bullet points with fully extended, well-judged
  content. Purpose is entirely clear; tone is consistent and completely appropriate
  to the reader throughout.
- **Band 8** — Covers all three bullet points sufficiently with well-extended
  content. Clear purpose; tone is appropriate and consistent, with at most very
  occasional lapses.
- **Band 7** — Covers all three bullet points and presents a clear purpose, though
  one point may be less developed than the others. Tone is generally appropriate and
  consistent, with occasional lapses that do not confuse the reader.
- **Band 6** — Addresses the task and covers the bullet points, but one may be
  underdeveloped or unclear; purpose is generally clear. Tone is generally
  appropriate but **inconsistent in places** (e.g. an informal phrase in a formal
  letter).
- **Band 5** — Generally addresses the task but the **format may be inappropriate**;
  one bullet point may be omitted or barely touched. Purpose may be unclear in
  places. **Tone is inappropriate for the reader or noticeably inconsistent** (no
  overview-equivalent: an unsuitable/wavering register caps TA at 5).
- **Band 4** — Attempts the task but one or more bullet points are missing or
  misunderstood; the format is inappropriate. Purpose is unclear and tone is
  inappropriate throughout.

> **Register note.** A consistent register is to GT Task 1 what the overview is to
> Academic Task 1 — the criterion-defining feature. Judge it against the
> relationship the prompt sets up (official/stranger ⇒ formal; acquaintance ⇒
> semi-formal; friend/family ⇒ informal), and against the chosen opening/closing
> (e.g. "Dear Sir or Madam … Yours faithfully" vs "Hi Sam … See you soon").

## CC — Coherence and Cohesion

- **Band 9** — Cohesion attracts no attention; paragraphing is skilfully managed.
- **Band 8** — Sequences ideas logically; manages all aspects of cohesion well;
  paragraphing is appropriate (one purpose/point per paragraph).
- **Band 7** — Logically organised with clear progression; a range of cohesive
  devices used appropriately, with at most minor under-/over-use; clear paragraphs.
- **Band 6** — Coherent overall progression, but cohesion within/between sentences
  can be faulty or mechanical; referencing may not always be clear; paragraphing may
  not always be logical.
- **Band 5** — Some organisation but no clear overall progression; cohesive devices
  inadequate, inaccurate or over-used; paragraphing may be missing.
- **Band 4** — Not arranged coherently; basic cohesive devices used inaccurately or
  repetitively; paragraphing absent or confusing.

## LR — Lexical Resource

GT Task 1 rewards the **functional language of the letter's purpose** — requesting
("I would be grateful if you could…"), apologising, complaining, inviting — pitched
at the right level of formality.

- **Band 9** — Wide range used naturally with sophisticated control; rare slips only.
- **Band 8** — Wide resource used fluently and flexibly to convey precise meaning,
  including appropriate fixed/semi-fixed expressions for the function; rare
  spelling/word-formation errors.
- **Band 7** — Sufficient range for some flexibility and precision; some less common
  items and functional phrases with awareness of collocation and register;
  occasional errors in word choice, spelling or word formation.
- **Band 6** — Adequate range; attempts less common vocabulary and functional
  phrases but with some inaccuracy or register mismatch; some spelling/word-formation
  errors that do not impede communication.
- **Band 5** — Limited range, minimally adequate; relies on basic words and
  formulaic phrases; noticeable spelling/word-formation errors causing some
  difficulty.
- **Band 4** — Only basic vocabulary, used repetitively or inappropriately; limited
  control of word formation/spelling; errors may strain the reader.

## GRA — Grammatical Range and Accuracy

- **Band 9** — Wide range used with full flexibility and control; rare slips only.
- **Band 8** — Wide range of structures; the majority of sentences are error-free,
  with only very occasional errors or inappropriacies.
- **Band 7** — A variety of complex structures; frequent error-free sentences; good
  control of grammar and punctuation, though a few errors persist.
- **Band 6** — A mix of simple and complex forms; some errors in grammar and
  punctuation, but they rarely reduce communication.
- **Band 5** — Limited range; complex sentences are less accurate than simple ones;
  frequent errors and faulty punctuation can cause some difficulty.
- **Band 4** — Very limited range; rarely uses subordinate clauses; errors
  predominate and punctuation is often faulty.

---

## Bands 0–3 (non-attempt / severe under-length)

- **Band 3** — Does not adequately address the task; bullet points are barely
  identifiable. Little or no organisation; very limited vocabulary/grammar with
  frequent meaning-impeding errors.
- **Band 2** — Barely responds; no organisation worth the name; almost no usable
  vocabulary or sentence control.
- **Band 1** — Wholly unrelated to the prompt, or so short there is essentially no
  assessable language.
- **Band 0** — Did not attempt, not in English, or copied the prompt.

## Word-count gate (applies to ALL criteria)

GT Task 1 expects **≥ 150 words**. Underlength is **not only a TA issue**: a short
letter cannot show *range* of cohesion, vocabulary, or grammar, so it caps **CC, LR
and GRA** as well — never score those on the strength of a correct fragment. Apply
the **length & attempt gate in `SKILL.md`**, note the underlength count in the
relevant `what_caps_it`, and remember a handful of words is a non-attempt (Band 1–2
across the board), **not** a Band 4.
