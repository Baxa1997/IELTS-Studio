# Anchor 02 — Band 7.5

Original, expert-verification pending. Use to anchor strong scripts.

## Prompt

In many countries the proportion of older people is steadily increasing. Some
people see this trend as a problem, while others believe it brings benefits to
society. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

## Essay (≈ 270 words)

It is true that the elderly now make up a growing share of the population in many
countries. While this demographic shift undoubtedly poses certain challenges, I
would argue that the contributions older citizens make outweigh the difficulties
they present.

Those who regard an ageing society as a problem tend to focus on its economic
cost. As people live longer, governments are obliged to spend heavily on pensions
and medical care, and this burden falls on a shrinking pool of working-age
taxpayers. Japan is a frequently cited example: its rapidly ageing population has
placed considerable strain on public finances and contributed to labour shortages
in several industries. These pressures are real and cannot be dismissed.

Nevertheless, I believe the benefits of having more older people are easy to
overlook. To begin with, the elderly represent an enormous reservoir of skill and
experience; retired professionals often mentor younger colleagues or return to
work part-time, passing on expertise that cannot be taught in a classroom.
Furthermore, grandparents frequently provide unpaid childcare, enabling both
parents to remain in employment and strengthening family bonds in the process.
Far from being a drain on resources, many older people actively sustain their
communities through volunteering.

In conclusion, although an ageing population creates genuine financial pressures,
these are outweighed by the wisdom, stability and practical support that older
generations offer. Rather than treating longer life as a crisis, societies would
do better to design policies that allow older citizens to keep contributing.
