# Task 1 Academic Anchor 01 — Band 5.5

Original, expert-verification pending. Use to anchor Academic Task 1 scripts that
report the data but give **no clear overview** (the single feature that caps TA at
Band 5).

## Prompt

The chart below shows the percentage of households that owned a car in four cities
in 2010 and 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main
features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

## Figure (the ground truth — data the candidate must report accurately)

Bar chart — % of households owning a car, 2010 vs 2020:

| City | 2010 | 2020 |
|------|------|------|
| Aville | 45% | 60% |
| Beston | 70% | 72% |
| Carton | 30% | 55% |
| Dunwich | 65% | 50% |

(Accurate summary: ownership rose in three cities — most sharply in Carton and
Aville — but fell in Dunwich; Beston was highest in 2010 and barely changed.)

## Essay (≈ 155 words)

The bar chart show the percentage of households that own a car in four different
cities in the year 2010 and 2020.

In Aville, the number was 45% in 2010 and it increase to 60% in 2020. For Beston,
the percentage was 70% in 2010 and 72% in 2020, so it almost the same. In Carton,
only 30% of households had a car in 2010, but in 2020 this number become 55%. In
Dunwich, the percentage was 65% in 2010 and after that it go down to 50% in 2020.

So we can see that some cities have more cars and some cities have less cars.
Beston is the city with the highest percentage in 2010. In conclusion, the car
ownership is different in each city and it change from 2010 to 2020.

## Calibrated bands — overall **5.5**

- **TA 5** — All the figures are reported and they are accurate, but there is **no
  clear overview**: the script recounts each city in turn and the final sentence
  ("different in each city") is too vague to count as a summary of the main trends.
  No overview ⇒ TA cannot exceed Band 5.
- **CC 6** — Arranged coherently with clear overall progression (city by city), but
  the data is not grouped (e.g. risers vs the faller) and cohesion is mechanical
  ("So we can see", "In conclusion").
- **LR 6** — Adequate but repetitive: the same few items recur ("percentage",
  "number", "the city") and the language of change is limited ("increase", "go
  down", "become") (LR-BASIC).
- **GRA 5** — Frequent errors, mostly tense and agreement: "chart show", "it
  increase", "it almost the same" (missing verb), "this number become", "it go
  down", "it change". They are dense enough to cause some difficulty (GRA-AGREEMENT,
  GRA-TENSE, GRA-DENSITY).

## Score-blocker

**TA + GRA** — the missing overview holds Task Achievement at 5, and frequent
verb-form errors hold grammar at 5; the data itself is reported accurately.

## Band with fixes → ~6.5

Add a real overview sentence after the introduction (ownership rose in three cities
but fell in Dunwich; Beston changed least), group the cities by trend, and fix the
present/past tense and subject–verb agreement.
