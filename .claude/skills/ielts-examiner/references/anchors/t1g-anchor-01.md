# Task 1 General Anchor 01 — Band 5.0

Original, expert-verification pending. Use to anchor a modest GT letter: the task is
generally addressed and the three bullet points are all touched, but development is
thin, the register wavers, vocabulary is basic/repetitive and grammatical errors are
frequent enough to cause the reader some difficulty.

## Prompt

You recently stayed at a hotel and were unhappy with the service. Write a letter to
the hotel manager. In your letter:
- explain why you were staying at the hotel
- describe the problems you experienced
- say what you would like the manager to do

Write at least 150 words. (Formal — you do not know the manager.)

## Letter (≈ 155 words)

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing about your hotel. I stayed in your hotel last week and I want to tell
you the problems.

First, the room was very dirty when I arrived. The bed sheets they were not clean and
the bathroom had no hot water for three days. I told the reception but nobody come to
fix it. Also the breakfast was cold every morning and the staff was not friendly to
me, this made me very angry.

I think it is not good for a hotel like this. I paid a lot of money and I did not get
a good service. So I want you to give me back some money because of these problems.
Please reply me soon.

Thank you,
Mr Karimov

## Calibrated bands — overall **5.0**

- **TA 5** — All three areas are touched, but the reason for the stay is barely given
  (one half-sentence), and the requested action is vague ("give me back some money").
  The purpose is clear, yet the content is underdeveloped, and the register slips from
  formal into the conversational ("this made me very angry"), which keeps it at 5.
- **CC 5** — There is some organisation and a few basic connectives ("First", "Also",
  "So"), but progression is little more than a list, referencing is faulty ("The bed
  sheets they were not clean"), and the closing is abrupt.
- **LR 5** — Limited, repetitive vocabulary ("problems", "very", "good") that is only
  minimally adequate for the task; the functional language of a complaint is absent.
- **GRA 5** — Frequent errors in a mostly simple range: "nobody come", "the staff was
  not friendly", "Please reply me", and the doubled subject above. They do not block
  meaning but cause the reader difficulty.

## Score-blocker

LR and GRA together — the basic, repetitive lexis and the density of grammatical slips
hold the letter at 5; the content is present but thinly developed.

## Band with fixes → ~6.0

Sustain a formal register throughout, replace the basic verbs with the functional
language of a complaint ("I am writing to complain about…", "I would be grateful if
you could refund…"), and fix the recurring subject/verb and reference errors.
