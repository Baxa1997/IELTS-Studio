# Task 1 Academic Anchor 04 — Band 4.5

Original, expert-verification pending. Use to anchor the **floor** for Academic
Task 1: a full-length attempt that recounts the data mechanically with **no
overview**, almost no language of comparison, and dense agreement/plural errors.

## Prompt

The bar chart below shows the number of visitors to four attractions in a city in
2019. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and
make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

## Figure (the ground truth — data the candidate must report accurately)

Bar chart — visitors in 2019, in millions:

| Attraction | Visitors (millions) |
|------------|---------------------|
| Museum | 4.5 |
| Zoo | 3.0 |
| Art gallery | 2.0 |
| Botanical garden | 1.5 |

(Accurate summary: the museum was by far the most visited (4.5m), followed by the
zoo (3.0m) and the gallery (2.0m); the botanical garden was the least visited
(1.5m) — the museum drew three times as many visitors as the garden.)

## Essay (≈ 155 words)

The bar chart give information about the number of visitor who go to four attraction
in a city in 2019, the unit is million.

In the chart we can see the museum. The museum have 4.5 million visitor and it is a
big number. After that the zoo have 3 million visitor in the year 2019. The art
gallery is also in the chart and it have 2 million visitor which is not so much. The
last one is the botanical garden, the botanical garden have only 1.5 million visitor
and it is the smallest.

The museum is more popular than the garden. The zoo and the gallery is in the
middle. The people like to visit the museum and the zoo more than the other place.

So we can see different attraction have different number of visitor in this city.

## Calibrated bands — overall **4.5**

- **TA 5** — All four figures are reported and are accurate, but there is **no
  clear overview** — the data is recounted bar by bar with minimal selection and
  the closing line is a vague generality, not an overview. (No overview ⇒ TA cannot
  exceed Band 5.)
- **CC 5** — Some organisation and a discernible sequence ("After that", "The last
  one", "So"), but progression is weak, the overview is absent rather than
  separated, and referencing is clumsily repetitive ("the botanical garden, the
  botanical garden") (CC-REFERENCING).
- **LR 4** — Only basic vocabulary, heavily repetitive ("have ... visitor",
  "popular", "number"); almost none of the language of trend, proportion or
  comparison the task rewards, and prompt words are recycled (LR-BASIC).
- **GRA 4** — Errors predominate: subject–verb agreement fails repeatedly ("give",
  "the museum have", "the zoo and the gallery is"), plurals are dropped
  ("visitor", "attraction"), and a comma splice opens the script. Range is very
  limited (GRA-AGREEMENT, GRA-DENSITY).

## Score-blocker

**LR & GRA (with the missing overview on TA)** — basic, repetitive vocabulary with
no comparison language and dense agreement/plural errors hold the script to 4.5;
the absent overview independently caps TA at 5.

## Band with fixes → ~5.5

garden least) and group the data with comparison language ("three times as many
as", "the least popular"); fix the subject–verb agreement and the missing plurals.
