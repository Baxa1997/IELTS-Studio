## Prompt

Many people believe that international tourism harms the environment and local
cultures. What are the causes of this problem, and what measures could reduce it?

## Essay (≈ 260 words)

International tourism has grown very fast in the last decades, and many people are
worried that it harms both the nature and the local culture of the places that
tourists visit. In this essay I will look at the main causes of this problem and
suggest some solutions.

There are two main causes. Firstly, the large number of tourists puts a big
pressure on popular destinations. Cities like Venice receive millions of visitors
every year, which creates a lot of pollution and rubbish and damages old buildings
and the environment around them. Secondly, when a place becomes too dependent on
the tourism, the local traditions are often changed into shows just for entertain
the visitors, so the real culture slowly loses its meaning.

There are also some measures that can reduce these problems. One solution is that
the government should control the number of tourists who enter the sensitive areas,
for example by selling a limited number of tickets each day. This would protect the
environment and also the historical places. Another solution is to use the money
from tourism to support local people and to repair the cultural sites, instead of
giving all the profit to big foreign companies.

In conclusion, tourism can bring serious damage to the environment and the local
culture, but these effects are not impossible to control. If governments manage
visitor numbers carefully and invest the income in the community, tourism and
tradition can exist together.
