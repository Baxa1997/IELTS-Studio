# Task 1 Academic Anchor 02 — Band 6.5

Original, expert-verification pending. Use to anchor competent Academic Task 1
scripts with a clear overview and accurate data, capped by repetitive lexis and a
few grammar slips.

## Prompt

The graph below shows the sales of three types of drink in one country between
2000 and 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main
features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

## Figure (the ground truth — data the candidate must report accurately)

Line graph — sales in millions of litres:

| Year | Bottled water | Carbonated drinks | Fruit juice |
|------|---------------|-------------------|-------------|
| 2000 | 50 | 200 | 90 |
| 2005 | 80 | 190 | 110 |
| 2010 | 120 | 160 | 120 |
| 2015 | 170 | 130 | 115 |
| 2020 | 210 | 100 | 105 |

(Accurate summary: bottled water rose steadily to become the most popular;
carbonated drinks fell continuously from the highest; the two reversed positions
around 2010; fruit juice rose to a peak then dipped slightly.)

## Essay (≈ 175 words)

The line graph illustrates how the sales of three types of drink — bottled water,
carbonated drinks and fruit juice — changed in one country between 2000 and 2020,
measured in millions of litres.

Overall, the sales of bottled water increased a lot over the period and it became
the most popular drink, while carbonated drinks did the opposite and went down
continuously. Fruit juice stayed in the middle for most of the time.

In 2000, carbonated drinks was the most popular drink, with sales of 200 million
litres, compared with only 50 million for bottled water. After that, bottled water
increased every year and reached 210 million litres in 2020, while carbonated
drinks dropped to just 100 million. The two drinks swapped their position at around
2010.

Fruit juice was less popular. Its sales grew from 90 million litres in 2000 to 120
million in 2010, but then they decreased slightly to 105 million at the end of the
period.
