# Anchor 01 — Band 6.0

Original, expert-verification pending. Use to anchor mid-band scripts.

## Prompt

In many countries the proportion of older people is steadily increasing. Some
people see this trend as a problem, while others believe it brings benefits to
society. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

## Essay (≈ 250 words)

Nowadays, the number of elderly people is growing in many nations. While some
people think that this is a serious problem for society, others argue that old
people can give many benefits. In my opinion, both sides are true, but I believe
the benefits are more important.

On the one hand, there are some problems with ageing population. Firstly, the
government must spend a lot of money on pensions and health care, because old
people often get sick and they need more treatment. This is a big pressure for
the economy. Secondly, when there are many retired people, there are not enough
workers, so the country can lose productivity and the young generation must pay
higher taxes.

On the other hand, elderly people also bring advantages. They have a lot of
experience and knowledge, which they can share with young people. For example,
grandparents can take care of children while parents are working, so the family
can save money for childcare. Also, many old people do volunteer work in their
community, which is very helpful for everyone.

In conclusion, although ageing population create some difficulties for the
government, I think that older people are valuable for the society because of
their experience and their help to families. Therefore, the government should
support them instead of seeing them as a burden.

## Calibrated bands — overall **6.0**

  generic examples ("save money for childcare") without real extension
  (TR-UNDERDEV).
- **CC 6** — Clear overall progression and real paragraphs, but the linking is
  mechanical ("Firstly… Secondly… On the one hand… In conclusion") and cohesion
  is formulaic rather than natural (CC-MECHANICAL).
- **LR 6** — Adequate, mostly common vocabulary with a few attempts at range
  ("productivity", "volunteer work"); some imprecision but nothing that blocks
  meaning (LR-BASIC).
- **GRA 6** — Mix of simple and complex sentences with errors that don't impede
  communication: subject–verb agreement ("ageing population create"), occasional
  missing articles (GRA-AGREEMENT, GRA-ARTICLE).
