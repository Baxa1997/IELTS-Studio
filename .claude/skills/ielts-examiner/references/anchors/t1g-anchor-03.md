# Task 1 General Anchor 03 — Band 7.5

Original, expert-verification pending. Use to anchor a strong GT letter: all three
points fully extended, a consistent and well-judged formal register, a good range of
less-common functional language, and frequent error-free complex sentences. The cap to
8 is that some of the phrasing is slightly formulaic rather than fully flexible.

## Prompt

You bought a product from an online shop and it arrived damaged. Write a letter to the
company. In your letter:
- explain what you bought and when
- describe the problem with the product
- say what you would like the company to do

Write at least 150 words. (Formal — you do not know the reader.)

## Letter (≈ 175 words)

Dear Customer Service Team,

I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with an order I received from your website
and to request a suitable resolution.

On the 3rd of March, I purchased a stainless-steel coffee machine (order number 48217)
from your online store, which was advertised as brand new. Unfortunately, when the
parcel arrived two days later, the machine was clearly damaged: the outer casing was
dented and the glass carafe had shattered in transit, most likely because of
inadequate packaging.

As the item is unusable in its current condition, I would be grateful if you could
either dispatch a replacement at no extra cost or issue a full refund, whichever can be
arranged more quickly. I have kept the original packaging and would be happy to return
the faulty unit once a collection has been organised.

I would appreciate a response within seven working days. I have been a loyal customer
for several years and hope this matter can be resolved promptly.

Yours faithfully,
Helen Carter

## Calibrated bands — overall **7.5**

- **TA 8** — All three points are covered and well extended, with precise, well-chosen
  detail (date, order number, the exact nature of the damage, a clear two-option
  request and an offer to return the unit). Purpose and formal register are sustained
  throughout.
- **CC 8** — Sequenced logically with smooth, unobtrusive cohesion ("Unfortunately…",
  "As the item is unusable…", "whichever can be arranged more quickly"); paragraphing
  is well judged.
- **LR 7** — A good range with appropriate less-common items ("dissatisfaction",
  "inadequate packaging", "dispatch a replacement", "resolved promptly"), but several
  turns are standard business formulae ("express my dissatisfaction", "suitable
  resolution") rather than fully flexible, original precision — the visible cap.
- **GRA 8** — A wide range of structures with the majority of sentences error-free and
  controlled punctuation, including the colon and parenthetical aside; only the slight
  effortfulness that separates this from Band 9.

## Score-blocker

LR — everything else sits at 8, but the lexis, while strong and accurate, leans on
familiar business phrasing, so Lexical Resource keeps the overall band at 7.5.

## Band with fixes → ~8.0

Replace the formulaic openers with more flexible, precise phrasing and vary the
sentence openings a little more; task, coherence and grammar are already at the top of
the band.
