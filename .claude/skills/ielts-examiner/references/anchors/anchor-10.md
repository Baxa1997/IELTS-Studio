## Calibrated bands — overall **3.0**

- **TR 3** — Does not adequately address the actual task: the advantages are never
  weighed against the disadvantages and no position ("outweigh" or not) is reached.
  Ideas are hard to identify and drift off-topic (the grandmother, "the world is
  changing"); support is minimal (TR-POSITION, TR-IRRELEVANT).
- **CC 3** — Little organisation and no clear progression; sentences are strung
  together with faulty cohesion and doubled subjects ("my grandmother she", "my
  friend he", "the people they"); paragraphing does not reflect any line of
  argument (CC-NOPROGRESSION, CC-CHOPPY).
- **LR 3** — Very limited vocabulary with combinations that obscure meaning ("Online
  is have many thing", "the money is can be danger", "both is have the thing good");
  errors are frequent enough to impede, not just distract (LR-BASIC).
- **GRA 3** — Errors predominate AND frequently impede meaning: broken verb phrases
  ("is have", "is can be"), dropped/duplicated subjects, missing articles, and no
  control of basic clause structure (GRA-DENSITY, GRA-AGREEMENT).

## Score-blocker

**GRA (with TR)** — pervasive errors that obscure meaning, and a task that is not
genuinely addressed (no evaluation, no position). The reader has to work to follow
the writer's point.

## Band with fixes → ~4.5

Answer the actual question — state whether the advantages outweigh the
disadvantages, and why — and cut the off-topic opening. Write one idea per short,
clear sentence; remove the doubled subjects and repair the broken verb phrases
("there are many things online", "money can be unsafe online").
