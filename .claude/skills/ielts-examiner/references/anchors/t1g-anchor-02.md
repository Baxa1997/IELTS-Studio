# Task 1 General Anchor 02 — Band 6.5

Original, expert-verification pending. Use to anchor a competent GT letter: all three
bullet points are covered and extended, the register is consistent and appropriate,
and the grammar is largely accurate — but the vocabulary stays fairly common (little
genuinely less-common or precise lexis), which is the visible ceiling that keeps it
below 7.

## Prompt

You would like to take some time off work to deal with a personal matter. Write a
letter to your manager. In your letter:
- explain why you need the time off
- say how much time you need
- suggest how your work could be covered

Write at least 150 words. (Semi-formal — you know your manager.)

## Letter (≈ 165 words)

Dear Mr Thompson,

I am writing to request some time off from work because of a personal matter that I
have to deal with.

Recently, my mother has been quite ill, and because I am her only close relative, I
need to go to my hometown to look after her and take her to some medical appointments.
For this reason, I would like to ask for two weeks of leave, if possible starting from
the 15th of this month.

I know that my absence could cause some difficulty for the team. To help with this, I
would be happy to give my current projects to my colleague Sarah, who already knows
about them, and I will prepare notes before I leave. I can also be contacted by email
if anything urgent happens.

I would be grateful if you could consider my request. Please let me know if you need
more information.

Yours sincerely,
David

## Calibrated bands — overall **6.5**

- **TA 7** — All three points are covered and genuinely extended (the reason, a
  specific duration with a start date, and a concrete cover plan), and the semi-formal
  register is consistent and well judged for a manager.
- **CC 7** — Logically organised with clear progression and appropriate linking ("For
  this reason", "To help with this"); each paragraph has one purpose. Cohesion is good,
  if not yet effortless.
- **LR 6** — Adequate range with some functional phrasing ("I am writing to request…",
  "I would be grateful if you could…"), but much of the lexis is common ("quite ill",
  "look after", "give my projects" rather than "hand over"); little genuinely
  less-common vocabulary. This is the cap.
- **GRA 7** — A variety of structures with frequent error-free sentences and good
  control; only the odd plain construction stops it short of the wide range of Band 8.

## Score-blocker

LR — the letter is well organised and accurate, but the vocabulary rarely reaches for
less-common or more precise items, so Lexical Resource holds the overall band at 6.5.

## Band with fixes → ~7.0

Trade the common verbs for more precise, idiomatic functional language ("hand over my
responsibilities", "at your earliest convenience", "cover my duties in my absence")
while keeping the accuracy already shown; TA, CC and GRA are already at 7.
