## Prompt

In many countries, people are working longer hours and have less leisure time than
in the past. Why is this happening, and is it a positive or negative development?

## Essay (≈ 285 words)

In recent years, employees in many parts of the world have found themselves working
longer hours while enjoying less free time than previous generations did. This essay
will explain why this trend has emerged and argue that, on balance, it is a negative
development.

Two factors largely account for the erosion of leisure time. The first is economic
insecurity. As living costs — particularly housing — have risen faster than wages in
many countries, people feel compelled to work extra hours simply to maintain their
standard of living. The second is technological: smartphones and laptops have
blurred the boundary between the office and the home, so that emails and messages
now follow workers into evenings and weekends that were once their own.

Although longer hours can raise output in the short term, I believe the consequences
are predominantly harmful. At the individual level, chronic overwork is strongly
associated with stress, exhaustion and a range of health problems, and it leaves
little time for the relationships and interests that make life meaningful. At the
social level, exhausted employees are, paradoxically, often less productive and less
creative, while families suffer when parents are perpetually absent. A culture that
treats constant availability as a virtue ultimately undermines the very wellbeing
that work is supposed to support.

In conclusion, the decline in leisure time stems mainly from financial pressure and
the intrusion of technology into private life. While there may be short-term economic
gains, the damage to health, relationships and even long-term productivity makes
this, in my view, a clearly negative trend.
