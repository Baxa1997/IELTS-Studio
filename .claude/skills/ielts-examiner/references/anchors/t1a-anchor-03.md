# Task 1 Academic Anchor 03 — Band 7.5

## Prompt

The table below shows the average monthly spending of low-, middle- and
high-income households on four categories. Summarise the information by selecting
and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at
least 150 words.

## Figure (the ground truth — data the candidate must report accurately)

Table — average monthly household spending (US$):

| Category | Low income | Middle income | High income |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Housing | 600 | 1,100 | 2,000 |
| Food | 400 | 550 | 700 |
| Transport | 150 | 400 | 650 |
| Leisure | 80 | 300 | 900 |

(Accurate summary: spending rises with income in every category; housing is the
largest expense for all groups; leisure shows the widest gap between low and high
income; food varies least.)

## Essay (≈ 185 words)

The table compares the average amount of money, in US dollars, that low-, middle-
and high-income households spend each month on four categories: housing, food,
transport and leisure.

Overall, spending rises consistently with income in every category, and housing is
the single largest expense for all three groups. The most striking contrast lies in
leisure, where high-income families spend far more than the others, whereas
spending on food varies relatively little.

Housing dominates each budget, climbing from $600 a month for low-income households
to $1,100 for middle-income ones and $2,000 for the wealthiest — more than three
times the lowest figure. Leisure shows an even sharper divide: low-income families
set aside just $80, compared with $900 for high-income families.

Food, by contrast, is the most stable category, ranging only from $400 to $700
across the three groups. Transport falls between these extremes, rising steadily
from $150 to $650 as household income increases.
