## Prompt

The line graph below shows the percentage of the population using the internet in
three countries between 2000 and 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and
reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least
150 words.

## Figure (the ground truth — data the candidate must report accurately)

| Year | Country A | Country B | Country C |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 2000 | 25 | 10 | 5 |
| 2005 | 45 | 25 | 12 |
| 2010 | 65 | 50 | 30 |
| 2015 | 80 | 70 | 55 |
| 2020 | 90 | 85 | 78 |

(Accurate summary: usage rose substantially in all three countries; Country A led

## Essay (≈ 195 words)

The line graph illustrates the proportion of the population using the internet in
three countries, labelled A, B and C, over the two decades from 2000 to 2020.

Overall, internet usage rose markedly in all three countries across the period.
Although Country A maintained the highest level of access throughout, the
substantial gap that had separated the three nations in 2000 narrowed considerably
by 2020, as the countries starting from a lower base grew at a faster rate.

In 2000, Country A was already well ahead, with a quarter of its population online,
compared with just 10 per cent in Country B and a mere 5 per cent in Country C. Over
the following fifteen years, Country A climbed steadily to 80 per cent, while
Country B rose more sharply to 70 per cent.

The most dramatic change occurred in Country C, where usage surged from the lowest
starting point to 78 per cent in 2020. By the end of the period the three figures
had almost converged — at 90, 85 and 78 per cent respectively — leaving only a
narrow margin between countries that had been widely disparate two decades earlier.
