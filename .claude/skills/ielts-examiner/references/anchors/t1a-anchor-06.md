## Prompt

The bar chart below shows the percentage of households owning four types of consumer
technology — smartphones, broadband internet, tablets and smart speakers — in one
country in 2010 and 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the
main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

| Smartphones        | 35   | 92   |

| Smart speakers     | 0    | 40   |

## Essay (≈ 195 words)

The bar chart compares the proportion of households owning four types of consumer
technology — smartphones, broadband internet, tablets and smart speakers — in a single
country in 2010 and 2020.

Overall, ownership of every technology increased over the decade, in some cases
steeply. The most striking development was the rise of the smartphone, which overtook
broadband to become the most widely owned device, while the smart speaker emerged from
complete absence to reach a substantial share of homes.

In 2010, broadband internet was by far the most common, present in 60 per cent of
households, followed at a distance by smartphones at 35 per cent and tablets at a mere
8 per cent; smart speakers, meanwhile, had yet to appear at all.

By 2020 this picture had been transformed. Smartphone ownership had surged to 92 per
cent, narrowly ahead of broadband, which had climbed more modestly to 88 per cent.
Tablet ownership had expanded markedly to 55 per cent, and the smart speaker —
non-existent a decade earlier — was by then found in two in five homes, a remarkable
advance for a technology starting from zero.
