# Band 9 — what it is, and how to write an authentic Band 9 model answer

Grounded in the **public official IELTS Writing band descriptors**. This is the
reference used both to (a) recognise a genuine Band 9 when grading and (b) write
the **Band 9 model answer** in the post-grade comparison panel so it sits *honestly*
at 9.0 — never an inflated 8 relabelled (CLAUDE.md: calibrated, no inflation).

Band 9 is **"fully operational command"** of written English: the expert user. It is
not "a perfect essay" or "the longest, most ornate essay" — it is writing in which
nothing on any criterion stops the reader, and any slip is the kind a careful native
writer might make. Crucially, **Band 9 reads effortlessly.** The single most common
way a real Band 8/8.5 falls short of 9 is that the control, though excellent, is
*slightly effortful* — a marginally less exact word, a sentence that could be tighter,
cohesion you faintly notice. At 9 you notice nothing.

---

## The four criteria at Band 9 (and the 8 → 9 line)

**Task Response / Achievement (TR / TA)**
- **9** — Fully addresses *all* parts of the task with a **fully developed** position;
  every idea is relevant, **fully extended** and **well supported**. Nothing feels
  thin, padded, or over-generalised.
- **vs 8** — An 8 *sufficiently* addresses all parts and is well-developed, but may
  *very occasionally* over-generalise or leave one idea slightly less extended than
  the rest. At 9 there is no such soft spot, and the conclusion follows inevitably
  from the argument rather than merely restating it.
- **Task 1:** a Band 9 overview captures the *truly* defining features (not just "it
  went up"); every figure quoted is accurate and well selected; comparisons are
  exactly the ones that matter.

**Coherence & Cohesion (CC)**
- **9** — Cohesion is used **so naturally it attracts no attention**; paragraphing is
  **skilfully managed**. You cannot point to a linking device as a "device" — the
  text simply flows.
- **vs 8** — An 8 manages all aspects of cohesion *well*, but you can still *see* the
  machinery ("Firstly… Secondly… In conclusion"). At 9 the progression is carried by
  the logic itself — reference, substitution and sentence connection are invisible.

**Lexical Resource (LR)**
- **9** — A wide range used **naturally** with **very sophisticated control**; the
  word is always the *exact* one; collocations are idiomatic; any error is a rare
  **slip**, not a gap in resource.
- **vs 8** — An 8 uses uncommon vocabulary *skilfully* but has *occasional*
  inaccuracies in word choice or collocation, or a phrase that is impressive yet
  slightly off. At 9 precision never lapses, and rarer items appear because the
  meaning demands them — never as decoration.

**Grammatical Range & Accuracy (GRA)**
- **9** — A wide range used with **full flexibility and control**; rare minor errors
  occur **only as slips**. The majority-error-free of Band 8 becomes essentially
  error-free, with structures (cleft sentences, fronting, controlled subordination,
  parenthetical asides) deployed for *effect*, not just for variety.
- **vs 8** — An 8 has a wide range with the *majority* of sentences error-free and
  *very occasional* errors/inappropriacies. At 9 the complex structures carry no cost
  in accuracy — flexibility and correctness hold together.

## How to WRITE a genuine Band 9 model answer

1. **Argument first.** Pick a single, clear, debatable position (Task 2) or the
   genuinely defining trend (Task 1) and let everything serve it. A Band 9 is won on
   *thought*, then expressed flawlessly — not by stacking fancy words on a thin point.
2. **Fully extend every idea.** Each body paragraph: a clear claim, a real reason, and
   a concrete consequence or example that *earns* the claim. No idea is left as an
   assertion.
3. **Make cohesion invisible.** Prefer pronoun reference, this/such, and logical
   sequencing over signpost connectives. If a "Firstly/However" can be removed without
   loss, remove it. Paragraphs should open on their own topic, not on a connector.
4. **Choose the exact word, not the rare word.** Reach for an uncommon item only when
   it is *more precise* than the common one ("a by-product", "perverse", "obsolete",
   "confer", "tolerate uncertainty"). Idiomatic collocation > showy vocabulary.
5. **Vary structure for effect with full accuracy.** Use cleft sentences ("It is
   precisely this freedom that…"), participle and relative clauses, and the occasional
   short sentence for emphasis — but never at the cost of a single error.
6. **Length:** Task 2 ≈ 270–300 words, Task 1 ≈ 180–210 — enough to fully develop,
   never padded. A Band 9 is concise *because* every sentence does work.
7. **Read it back for effortlessness.** If any sentence feels effortful, a word feels
   approximate, or you *notice* a linker — it is an 8.5, not a 9. Fix it or do not
   call it a 9.

## Honesty guard (when generating the comparison samples)

The Band 9 sample must be a *real* 9 by the criteria above, and the Band 8 and Band 7
samples beneath it must show a **visible, learnable gap** down from it — so the learner
can see what "effortless and exact" looks like next to "excellent but slightly
effortful" (8) and "good with a clear ceiling" (7). Never label a strong 8 as a 9.
Use only original content; never reproduce copyrighted test material or published
model essays.
