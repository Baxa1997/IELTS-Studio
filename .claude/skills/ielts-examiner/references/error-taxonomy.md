# Error Taxonomy — what caps each criterion

The faults that hold a script below the next band. Use this to fill each
criterion's `what_caps_it` and to choose the **score-blocker**. Every claim must
point to evidence in the essay — do not flag a fault you cannot quote.

This is **our own** taxonomy (not copied from any test book). Treat it as the
checklist behind the descriptors, not a substitute for them.

---

## TR — Task Response

| Code | Fault | Typically caps at |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| TR-WRONGTYPE | Answers a different question than the one set (e.g. gives an opinion when asked to discuss both views; ignores half a two-part prompt) | 5–6 |
| TR-TEMPLATE | Memorised template scaffolding / pre-learned padding that fits any topic — not genuine, on-prompt development | 4 if substantially memorised; 5–6 for partial scaffolding |
| TR-PARTS | One part of a multi-part prompt ignored or barely touched | 6 |
| TR-POSITION | Position unclear, drifting, or self-contradictory | 5–6 |
| TR-UNDERDEV | Main ideas asserted but not extended/explained | 6 |
| TR-NOEXAMPLE | No specific support; only generalities | 6 |
| TR-OVERGEN | Sweeping over-generalisation used as evidence | 7 |
| TR-IRRELEVANT | Off-topic or padding content | 5 |
| TR-NOCONCLUSION | Missing or contentless conclusion | 6 |
| TR-UNDERLENGTH | Under 250 words → position/ideas cannot be fully developed | 5–6 |

## CC — Coherence and Cohesion

| Code | Fault | Typically caps at |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| CC-NOPROGRESSION | No clear overall progression of argument | 5 |
| CC-PARAGRAPH | No paragraphs, or paragraphs without one clear central topic | 5–6 |
| CC-MECHANICAL | Linkers present but mechanical/formulaic ("Firstly… Secondly… In conclusion") | 6 |
| CC-OVERUSE | Cohesive devices over-used or misused | 5–6 |
| CC-REFERENCING | Unclear pronoun/reference, forcing re-reading | 6 |
| CC-CHOPPY | Faulty cohesion within/between sentences | 6 |

## LR — Lexical Resource

| Code | Fault | Typically caps at |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| LR-BASIC | Vocabulary basic/repetitive for the topic | 5 |
| LR-COLLOCATION | Wrong collocations ("do a mistake", "make research") | 6–7 |
| LR-WORDCHOICE | Imprecise word choice blurring meaning | 6 |
| LR-FORMATION | Word-formation errors (wrong part of speech) | 6 |
| LR-SPELLING | Spelling errors noticeable enough to distract | 5–6 |
| LR-REGISTER | Informal/inappropriate register for academic essay | 6 |
| LR-MEMORISED | Memorised "big words" misused, showing no real control | 6 |

## GRA — Grammatical Range and Accuracy

| Code | Fault | Typically caps at |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| GRA-SIMPLEONLY | Mostly simple sentences; little subordination | 5 |
| GRA-COMPLEXFAIL | Complex attempts markedly less accurate than simple ones | 5–6 |
| GRA-ARTICLE | Persistent article errors (a/an/the/zero) | 6 |
| GRA-AGREEMENT | Subject–verb / number agreement errors | 6 |
| GRA-TENSE | Inconsistent or wrong tense | 6 |
| GRA-PUNCT | Faulty punctuation (run-ons, comma splices, missing stops) | 6 |
| GRA-DENSITY | Errors frequent enough to cause the reader difficulty | 5 |

---

## How to use it

1. For each criterion, find the **lowest-numbered fault present** with real
   evidence — that is the realistic ceiling for the criterion.
2. The **score-blocker** is the criterion whose ceiling is dragging the overall
   band down the most. Name the code's fault in plain language in `why`.
3. The **fix** for each criterion is the single change that lifts it past its
   current cap — concrete and tied to the evidence, not generic advice.
