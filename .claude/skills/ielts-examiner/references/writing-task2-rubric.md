# Writing Task 2 — Band Descriptors (4–9)

Grounded in the **public official IELTS Writing Task 2 band descriptors**,
restated in structured form for grading use. Four criteria, weighted equally:

- **TR** — Task Response
- **CC** — Coherence and Cohesion
- **LR** — Lexical Resource
- **GRA** — Grammatical Range and Accuracy

The overall band is the **mean of the four, rounded to the nearest 0.5**. On a
tie between two bands, **round down** (see `SKILL.md`).

---

## TR — Task Response

- **Band 9** — Fully addresses all parts of the task. Presents a fully developed
  position with relevant, fully extended and well-supported ideas.
- **Band 8** — Sufficiently addresses all parts. Presents a well-developed
  response with relevant, extended and supported ideas. (May very occasionally
  over-generalise.)
- **Band 7** — Addresses all parts, though some more fully than others. Presents a
  clear position throughout. Main ideas are extended and supported, but there may
  be a tendency to over-generalise or some ideas may lack focus.
- **Band 6** — Addresses all parts, though some more fully covered than others.
  Presents a relevant position, but conclusions may be unclear, repetitive, or
  not fully drawn. Main ideas are relevant but some are insufficiently developed
  or unclear.
- **Band 5** — Addresses the task only partially; the format may be inappropriate
  in places. Expresses a position but development is not always clear and there
  may be no conclusions drawn. Main ideas are limited and insufficiently
  developed; there may be irrelevant detail.
- **Band 4** — Responds minimally or tangentially; the format may be
  inappropriate. The position is unclear. Few ideas, which are difficult to
  identify, may be repetitive, irrelevant or unsupported.

## CC — Coherence and Cohesion

- **Band 9** — Uses cohesion so naturally it attracts no attention. Paragraphing
  is skilfully managed.
- **Band 8** — Sequences information and ideas logically. Manages all aspects of
  cohesion well. Paragraphing is used sufficiently and appropriately.
- **Band 7** — Logically organises information and ideas with clear progression
  throughout. Uses a range of cohesive devices appropriately, though there may be
  some under-/over-use. Each paragraph has a clear central topic.
- **Band 6** — Arranges information coherently with clear overall progression.
  Uses cohesive devices effectively, but cohesion within and/or between sentences
  can be faulty or mechanical. Referencing may not always be clear. Paragraphing
  may not always be logical.
- **Band 5** — Presents information with some organisation, but no clear overall
  progression. Cohesive devices are inadequate, inaccurate or over-used.
  Referencing/substitution is weak, causing repetition. Paragraphing may be
  missing or inadequate.
- **Band 4** — Information and ideas are not arranged coherently and there is no
  clear progression. Uses some basic cohesive devices, but inaccurately or
  repetitively. Paragraphing may be absent or confusing.

## LR — Lexical Resource

- **Band 9** — Wide range used naturally with very sophisticated control; rare
  minor errors occur only as slips.
- **Band 8** — Wide resource used fluently and flexibly to convey precise
  meaning. Skilful use of uncommon items, with only occasional inaccuracies in
  word choice/collocation. Rare errors in spelling/word formation.
- **Band 7** — Sufficient range for some flexibility and precision. Uses less
  common items with some awareness of style and collocation. Occasional errors in
  word choice, spelling or word formation.
- **Band 6** — Adequate range for the task. Attempts less common vocabulary but
  with some inaccuracy. Some errors in spelling/word formation, but they do not
  impede communication.
- **Band 5** — Limited range, minimally adequate for the task. Noticeable errors
  in spelling and/or word formation that may cause some difficulty for the reader.
- **Band 4** — Only basic vocabulary, used repetitively or inappropriately for the
  task. Limited control of word formation/spelling; errors may strain the reader.

## GRA — Grammatical Range and Accuracy

- **Band 9** — Wide range used with full flexibility and control; rare minor
  errors occur only as slips.
- **Band 8** — Wide range of structures. The majority of sentences are
  error-free, with only very occasional errors or inappropriacies.
- **Band 7** — Uses a variety of complex structures. Frequent error-free
  sentences. Good control of grammar and punctuation, but a few errors persist.
- **Band 6** — Mix of simple and complex sentence forms. Some errors in grammar
  and punctuation, but they rarely reduce communication.
- **Band 5** — Limited range of structures. Attempts complex sentences, but these
  are less accurate than simple ones. Frequent grammatical errors; punctuation may
  be faulty. Errors can cause some difficulty for the reader.
- **Band 4** — Very limited range; rarely uses subordinate clauses. Some accurate
  structures, but errors predominate and punctuation is often faulty.

## Bands 0–3 (non-attempt / severe under-length)

The table above starts at Band 4; below it, for scripts that barely respond:

- **Band 3** — Does not adequately address any part of the task; ideas are hard to
  identify and may be largely irrelevant. Little or no organisation. Control of
  vocabulary and grammar is very limited, with frequent errors that impede meaning.
- **Band 2** — Barely responds to the task. No organisation worth the name. Almost
  no usable vocabulary or sentence control; meaning is hard to access.
- **Band 1** — Answer is wholly unrelated, or so short (a few words / one sentence)
  that there is essentially no assessable language.
- **Band 0** — Did not attempt, not in English, or fully memorised / copied prompt.

## Word-count gate (applies to ALL criteria)

Task 2 expects **≥ 250 words** (Task 1 ≥ 150). Underlength is **not only a TR
issue**: a short script cannot show *range* of cohesion, vocabulary, or grammar, so
it caps **CC, LR and GRA** as well — never score those on the strength of a correct
fragment. Apply the **length & attempt gate in `SKILL.md`**, note the underlength
count in the relevant `what_caps_it`, and remember a handful of words is a
non-attempt (Band 1–2 across the board), **not** a Band 4.
