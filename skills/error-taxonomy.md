# Error Taxonomy

A fixed catalog of common issues, each mapped to the criterion it affects and a reusable fix template. Use these labels so feedback is **consistent across essays and students** — the same problem always gets the same name and the same kind of fix. This is what turns vague advice ("improve your coherence") into specific, trustable coaching.

Each entry: **ID · Name · Criterion · Signal · Fix template.**

---

## Task Response (TR)

- **TR-01 · Missing prompt part** · TR · Essay answers only one of two questions asked (e.g., discusses advantages but the prompt also asked for your opinion). · *Fix:* "The prompt asks you to [X] AND [Y]; you covered [X] but not [Y]. Add a paragraph addressing [Y]." → caps TR at 6.
- **TR-02 · Unclear/shifting position** · TR · Reader can't tell the writer's stance, or it changes between intro and conclusion. · *Fix:* "State your position in one sentence in the intro and restate it in the conclusion. Right now your intro leans [A] but your conclusion leans [B]."
- **TR-03 · Underdeveloped idea** · TR · A main point is asserted but not explained or exemplified. · *Fix:* "Develop [idea] with a 'because…' and one concrete example. One sentence isn't enough to support a main point."
- **TR-04 · Overgeneralization / no specifics** · TR · Claims like "everyone knows" with no concrete support. · *Fix:* "Replace the general claim with a specific example or mechanism."
- **TR-05 · Under length** · TR · Below ~250 words. · *Fix:* "You wrote [N] words; aim for 260–290. Under 250 is penalized regardless of quality."
- **TR-06 · Off-topic drift** · TR · Paragraph wanders from the question. · *Fix:* "This paragraph drifts to [topic]; tie it back to the exact question."

## Coherence & Cohesion (CC)

- **CC-01 · Linker overuse** · CC · "Moreover / Furthermore / In addition" stacked at every paragraph start. · *Fix:* "You used [linker] [N] times. Vary them and let some ideas connect through meaning, not a label." → caps CC at 6.
- **CC-02 · Mechanical/faulty linking** · CC · Linker used but the logical relationship is wrong ("However" between two agreeing sentences). · *Fix:* "'[linker]' signals [relationship], but these sentences [actual relationship]. Use [correct linker] or none."
- **CC-03 · Weak paragraphing** · CC · No clear one-topic-per-paragraph structure, or missing paragraph breaks. · *Fix:* "Give each body paragraph one central idea stated in the first sentence."
- **CC-04 · Referencing errors** · CC · "this/it/such" with an unclear antecedent. · *Fix:* "'This' refers to…? Make the reference explicit."
- **CC-05 · No progression** · CC · Ideas listed without logical flow. · *Fix:* "Order your points so each builds on the last; add a sentence linking paragraph [n] to [n+1]."

## Lexical Resource (LR)

- **LR-01 · Wrong word choice** · LR · A word that doesn't fit the meaning. · *Fix:* "'[word]' doesn't fit here; you mean '[better word]'."
- **LR-02 · Misused 'advanced' word** · LR · A rare word inserted incorrectly (thesaurus effect). · *Fix:* "'[word]' is misused — precision beats rarity. '[simpler accurate word]' scores higher than a wrong fancy one."
- **LR-03 · Collocation error** · LR · Unnatural pairing ("do a mistake", "strong rain"). · *Fix:* "Natural collocation: '[correct pairing]'."
- **LR-04 · Memorized chunk** · LR · Template phrase that doesn't show real range. · *Fix:* "'[chunk]' is a memorized phrase; examiners discount it. Express the idea in your own words."
- **LR-05 · Repetition** · LR · Same key word repeated where variation is expected. · *Fix:* "You repeat '[word]' [N] times; vary with [synonyms] where natural."
- **LR-06 · Word-form error** · LR · Wrong part of speech ("benefit" vs "beneficial"). · *Fix:* "Use the [noun/adjective/verb] form: '[correct form]'."

## Grammatical Range & Accuracy (GRA)

- **GRA-01 · Article error** · GRA · Missing/extra a/an/the. · *Fix:* "Add/remove the article: '[corrected]'."
- **GRA-02 · Subject–verb agreement** · GRA · "people is", "the data shows" vs intended. · *Fix:* "'[subject]' takes '[verb form]'."
- **GRA-03 · Tense inconsistency** · GRA · Unmotivated tense switching. · *Fix:* "Keep tense consistent; this paragraph mixes [tense A] and [tense B]."
- **GRA-04 · Faulty complex sentence** · GRA · Complex structure attempted but broken. · *Fix:* "The clause '[clause]' breaks down; restructure as '[fix]'." → if frequent, caps GRA at 6.
- **GRA-05 · Run-on / comma splice** · GRA · Two independent clauses joined by a comma. · *Fix:* "Split into two sentences or use a connector: '[fix]'."
- **GRA-06 · Limited range (simple-only)** · GRA · Only accurate simple sentences, no complex variety. · *Fix:* "Accurate but simple. Combine ideas with relative clauses / conditionals to show range." → caps GRA around 6.
- **GRA-07 · Punctuation** · GRA · Missing capitals, faulty commas. · *Fix:* "Punctuation: '[corrected]'."

---

## How to use in grading

For each issue you find, cite its ID in the per-criterion `evidence`. When the same ID appears repeatedly, that pattern is a strong candidate for the **score-blocker**. Over time, the frequency of each ID per student is exactly the data the dashboard tracks and the study plan targets.
