# Writing Task 2 — Operational Rubric

This is an **operational paraphrase** written for grading, not a reproduction of the official descriptors. Keep the official public IELTS Writing Task 2 band descriptors (published free by the IELTS partners) on hand as the canonical reference. Grade against this table, then sanity-check against the anchors in `anchors/`.

Bands 5–8 are described in detail because that's where almost all real essays sit. Band 9 = full, natural, error-free mastery across all four. Below 5 = frequent breakdowns in meaning.

---

## TR — Task Response

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **8** | Every part of the prompt is fully addressed. Position is clear and sustained from intro to conclusion. Ideas are well developed, relevant, and extended with specific support. No padding. |
| **7** | All parts addressed; clear position throughout. Main ideas are developed but one may be under-supported or slightly general. Minor over-generalization allowed. |
| **6** | Addresses the prompt but one part is covered weakly or unevenly. Position is present but may be unclear in places. Ideas are relevant but under-developed or repetitive; some support is vague. |
| **5** | Responds only partially; the position may be unclear or the format wrong (e.g., lists ideas without a stance). Main ideas are limited, repeated, or not well supported. **One part of the prompt ignored → cap here.** |

**Hard caps:** off-topic or a missing prompt-part caps TR at 6 (often 5). Under ~250 words caps TR. Memorized model-essay framing that doesn't fit the exact question lowers TR.

---

## CC — Coherence & Cohesion

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **8** | Effortless to follow. Logical progression, skillful paragraphing, cohesion so natural you don't notice it. Referencing (this, such, the latter) used accurately. |
| **7** | Clear overall progression and logical paragraphs. Cohesive devices used well but with occasional mechanical or slightly faulty use. Each paragraph has a clear central topic. |
| **6** | Information is arranged coherently and there's a clear overall progression, but cohesion within/between sentences can be faulty or mechanical. **Over-use of linkers** (Moreover/Furthermore stacked) belongs here. Paragraphing may be illogical or absent in places. |
| **5** | Some organization but it doesn't fully support the reader. Linking is inadequate, repetitive, or inaccurate. Paragraphing missing or unhelpful. |

**Key trap:** fluent, confident writing with repetitive or mechanical linking is **CC 6**, not 7. Fluency ≠ cohesion.

---

## LR — Lexical Resource

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **8** | Wide range used fluently and precisely, including less common and idiomatic items. Rare, non-impeding slips only. |
| **7** | Sufficient range to be flexible and precise. Some awareness of less common vocabulary and collocation; occasional errors in word choice or form that don't impede. |
| **6** | Adequate range for the task. Attempts less common vocabulary but with inaccuracy. Some errors in word choice/formation/spelling that don't impede communication. |
| **5** | Limited range, just adequate for the task. Noticeable errors in word choice/spelling that may cause some difficulty for the reader. Heavy reliance on basic vocabulary. |

**Calibration:** thesaurus-swapped "big words" used wrongly do **not** raise LR — they often lower it (precision matters more than rarity). Memorized lexical chunks ("plays a pivotal role", "double-edged sword") don't demonstrate range.

---

## GRA — Grammatical Range & Accuracy

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **8** | Wide range of structures. Majority of sentences error-free; only occasional, non-systematic slips. |
| **7** | Variety of complex structures. Frequent error-free sentences. Good control with some errors that don't impede communication. |
| **6** | Mix of simple and complex forms, but complex sentences tend to carry errors. Errors in grammar/punctuation occur but rarely reduce communication. |
| **5** | Limited range; complex structures are attempted but faulty. Errors are frequent and can cause the reader difficulty. Punctuation may be faulty. |

**Calibration:** a student who only writes accurate *simple* sentences is capped around GRA 6 — range matters. Frequent errors in complex sentences → 6, not 7, even if the ideas are good.

---

## Putting it together

1. Score each criterion against the table above with specific evidence.
2. Average the four; round to the nearest 0.5.
3. On any tie between two bands for a criterion, take the lower band and state the gap.
4. Name the single criterion most responsible for capping the overall band — that's the score-blocker.
