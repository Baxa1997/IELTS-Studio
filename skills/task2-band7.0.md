# Anchor — Task 2, Band 7.0 (original essay)

**Prompt:** Some people believe university students should pay the full cost of their studies, while others think higher education should be free for everyone. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

**Essay (as written by a band-7.0 candidate):**

> The question of who should fund higher education divides opinion. While some argue that students ought to cover the full cost of their degrees, others maintain that university should be free for all. This essay will examine both positions before explaining why I believe a partially subsidised system is fairest.
>
> Those who support full tuition fees usually point to the strain on public budgets. Governments that fund every student must either raise taxes or cut spending elsewhere, such as on healthcare. A further argument is that students who pay for their own education tend to take it more seriously; having invested a significant sum, they are more likely to attend lectures and complete their work rather than treat university as an extended holiday.
>
> On the other side, supporters of free education see it as a matter of equal opportunity. If degrees are expensive, talented students from poorer families may be priced out, which wastes their potential and widens inequality. Society also benefits when more people are qualified, because a skilled workforce attracts investment and raises productivity. From this perspective, education is an investment by the state rather than a private purchase.
>
> In my view, neither extreme is ideal. Charging the full cost discourages able students who lack money, but making everything free is difficult to sustain. A balanced approach — modest fees combined with means-tested grants and loans repaid only once graduates earn a decent salary — keeps universities funded while protecting access. This way, ability rather than wealth determines who studies.
